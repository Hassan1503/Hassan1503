About Me

Hello — I’m Hassan.

I am a PhD candidate in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Duke University, supported by an ECE departmental fellowship. I conduct research at the Kamaleswaran Lab under the supervision of Dr. Rishikesan Kamaleswaran, Associate Professor at Duke University School of Medicine.

What I work on

My research lies at the intersection of signal processing, deep learning, and healthcare AI, with a primary focus on developing scalable computational methods for the automated diagnosis of congenital heart diseases (CHD) using phonocardiogram (PCG) signals. I also work on multi-agent retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) systems and multimodal large language models (LLMs).

Before Duke

Prior to Duke, I completed my MSc in Digital Health at the University of Bristol, UK, where I was awarded the prestigious Think Big Postgraduate Scholarship. My undergraduate work and early research focused on biomedical signal analysis and the application of machine learning to clinical decision support.

Research Areas

What I Work On

I am broadly interested in computational methods that turn noisy biomedical signals and unstructured clinical data into reliable, scalable diagnostics. Current threads include:

Pediatric CHD Detection from Phonocardiograms Designing deep neural architectures (1D-CNNs, transformers) for automated screening of congenital heart disease from short-segment heart sounds across diverse, low-resource environments.
Biomedical Signal Processing Robust feature extraction, denoising, and segmentation pipelines for PCG, ECG, and other physiological time-series, including handcrafted-vs-learned feature fusion.
Multi-Agent RAG Systems Designing collaborating LLM agents that retrieve, reason over, and synthesize evidence from heterogeneous medical and scientific corpora.
Multimodal Large Language Models Joint reasoning over text, signals, and images for clinical narrative generation, decision support, and patient-facing applications.
Deep Learning for Biomedical Applications Transformer- and residual-based architectures for time-series classification with an emphasis on generalization, calibration, and deployment to resource-constrained settings.
Healthcare AI & Digital Health Translating models into clinically usable tools, with attention to fairness, robustness, and the realities of bedside data.
